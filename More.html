Conclusion and Findings

In general:
• Speaking of the total expenditures, the United States leads the way in all three expenditures; China followed closely and showed a trend of continuous growth.
• Due to the difference in population, China has low spending per capita; while Australia has high spending per capita as well as high GDP per capita.
• Relatively, Germany focuses more on education than military.

Military:
• South Korea attaches great importance to military development. It is understandable because of its proximity to North Korea.
• Russia is still trying to increase its military power because of its high military GDP ratio.

Healthcare:
• The 11 countries have not changed much in the proportion of health spending in GDP from 2012-2017
•Healthcare spending and GDP are dependent more or less.
• For some reason, 2015 was a trough in health spending and GDP for some countries(not including the United States and China)

Education:
• Countries have lower GDP may have higher education spending since education is represents the future of the country.
• Some countries like China is growing steadily in education spending; while some countries are doing the opposite.
